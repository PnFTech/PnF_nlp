Score My Essay

Introduction

Score your college admissions essay with TEssay's custom admissions essay word-scoring model. As a result of the on-going Data Science initiative in our Analytics branch we applied various natural language processing methods to an admissions essay corpus to generate a word-scoring model. Currently our model-training corpus is composed solely of essays written by Johns Hopkins University (JHU) admitted students.

Understanding The Model

Interpreting My Score

The main score to focus on is the Avg. Word Score. As the name implies, this score is the average score per word and is calculated by taking the total score and dividing by the total words (calculated and displayed directly below the essay input box). Think of this like a measure of word efficiency or density.

Once a score is calculated, you are now able to compare how close your writing style/content is to score data for different colleges shown in the table below. This will give you some idea of where your essay quality stands in comparison to the students admitted to the colleges below.
